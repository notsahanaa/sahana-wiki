Observations from AI dev con 2026

.

The Second Wave of AI (2026)
2025 (The first wave of AI) was about what is possible. 2026 (The second wave of AI) is about what is reliable and secure at scale. Here are a few problems that become important:
- Regulatory pressure (where data workloads and data they process physically reside)
- Latency reality (use cases like fraud detection, etc)
- Data gravity (moving data that lives in cloud, on prem etc to one place)

.

Characteristics that will be important in this AI future
- Tinkering. Jump in, build, figure it out
- Critical thinking. What to debug, where, when, how
- Taste. Is something good or not. This takes looking at a lot of patterns and developing judgement.
- Prioritization. Everyone takes about token maxing. What is most important is how we manage our time and spend it on what is important.
- Perspectives. Models are not the problem, they are getting better and creating new possibilities, knowing what to do with it and where this is going is.

.

The building blocks of agentic dev
Building agentic products is as good as assembling together a few "blocks". These blocks are:
- Agentic Blocks: LLM, Rag, agentic workflows, evals, error analysis, memory, context
- Non-agentic blocks: ui, auth, db
- Prod blocks: user empathy, rapid prototyping
