Digital Twins

One of the most exciting - but also scariest - stuff AI enables is digital twins.

I believe one of the reasons our current generation is much sharper than previous ones is the sheer exposure and knowledge at hand made possible due to the information era.

But AI, and particularly rag-based chat takes this to a whole new level. A project that I see a lot of people work on now is creating "digital/AI twins" of well known figures by training an AI on all of that person's talks, essays and other available online resources.

Some examples are:
Andrew Ng (AI): https://avatar.andrewng.org
Paul Graham: https://www.paulgraham-nia.com/

It is almost terrifying to think about what this implies. All the smartest people in today's generation can be codified and regenerated. They can be available as personalised mentors for almost everyone. Better still, we can train agents to take actions as they would, as agents become more powerful actors -- thereby creating almost clones. 

This is a conjecture ofc. Is a human merely the sum of their thoughts, words, speeches? Is there something more to judgement and intuition that cannot be grasped by these LLMs that train on their output? These are important questions that get borderline philosophical -- what does it mean to be a human, and a thinking-reasoning one at that?
