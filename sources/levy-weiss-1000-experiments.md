summary: >
  NFX partner Gigi Levy-Weiss argues that the paradigm shift from "software as tool" to "software
  as employee" demands a new founder psychology. With agents running in parallel, startups can
  orchestrate thousands of simultaneous experiments — which shifts the expected value of unconventional
  bets from reckless to rational. The math: a 5%-per-experiment signal probability reaches ~99% over
  100 tries. The key traits for the humans left at the center: high agency, multi-domain fluency,
  comfort with chaos, and willingness to unlearn.
