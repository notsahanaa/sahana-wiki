# Taste as the New Skill Gap

A claim from Sahana's *paradigms* essay (see [[projects/sahana-wiki]]): when everyone can build, **taste** becomes the differentiator. {{source:sahana-paradigms-agent-space-2026-04-12}}*"As everyone starts to build, 'taste' has become the new skill gap. The pop-culture references, movies tv shows and video games you geeked over, history and art classes in highschool, little personal quirks, albums and lyrics, and nostalgic trends suddenly make you a hot cake."*{{/source}}

This tracks with field observation: at AI Dev Con 2026, taste was named one of the five most important human characteristics in an AI-saturated future — {{source:ai-dev-con-2026-observations}}*"Is something good or not. This takes looking at a lot of patterns and developing judgement."*{{/source}} It sits alongside tinkering, critical thinking, prioritization, and perspective — all human-judgment capacities that models can't fully substitute.

## Builder as artist

The role expansion she observes: builders are taking on artist responsibilities. {{source:sahana-paradigms-agent-space-2026-04-12}}*"They are asking questions about aesthetics, fonts and animations, y2k and glitches. They are bringing back stories and histories from the archives of the internet to narrate with their products. Many products and websites have an almost rebellious angle to them; a setting, a persona, an experience that makes you feel like you belong to a certain ideology or cult."*{{/source}}

Products are no longer just utility — they're identity engagement.

## Aura as a deliverable

> {{source:sahana-paradigms-agent-space-2026-04-12}}*"Builders often go one stop ahead of the product they build itself. There's so much investment in 'aura'. Creating a killer demo video, adding photos from the build process, quotes lyrics and posters, and seeding their public pages with vibes. The process and the story of living through the product building is almost as important as the product now."*{{/source}}

People connect with builders, not just with their products. A robust public character — values, vibes, narratives — is now part of the work.

> {{source:sahana-paradigms-agent-space-2026-04-12}}*"Builders and techies as nerds and geeks is obsolete."*{{/source}}

## Systems for taste

{{source:sahana-paradigms-agent-space-2026-04-12}}*"There's so much investment in systems for art. Feeds and threads for cultivating taste, image and video generators, prompts and systems to teach ai taste, templates with stacks, ascii cards and web assets."*{{/source}}

This includes Kieran Klaassen's observation that {{source:klaassen-folder-is-the-agent}}folder-agents capture *taste* — personality and aesthetic preferences{{/source}} alongside knowledge. Taste is becoming an *artifact* that gets stored, shared, and inherited by AI systems — not just a quality of an individual maker.

## Voice vs. style — encoding taste

The cleanest method for actually doing this is in {{source:parrott-claude-every-standards}}Katie Parrott's *"I Taught Claude Every's Standards. It Taught Me Mine"*{{/source}}. The headline distinction:

> {{source:parrott-claude-every-standards}}*"Style is the 'how' — the choices of syntax, rhythm, and imagery that shape the prose. Voice is the 'why' — the convictions that give those stylistic choices meaning."*{{/source}}

Encoding standards into a Claude project forced Parrott to separate **personal preference** from **organizational value**. {{source:parrott-claude-every-standards}}*"I had to draw a line between my own preferences and Every's values, deciding which instincts deserved to become rules."*{{/source}} Some of her column's quirks shouldn't have been universal. Every rule needs a justification rooted in a principle, or it isn't a standard — it's a taste preference dressed up as one.

> {{source:parrott-claude-every-standards}}*"When you can teach taste to a machine, you're forced to make it legible for yourself."*{{/source}}

The encoding method, in compressed form:
1. Collect canonical exemplars.
2. Have a model surface recurring **structural patterns**.
3. Layer your own **core principles** on top (Every's: optimistic realism, intellectual generosity, conversational authority).
4. Run real drafts through the system; iterate where it produces "polished but wrong."

## Taste only counts if you exercise it

The complementary failure: {{source:parrott-ai-autopilot}}*"the machine making it feel as if you already thought."*{{/source}} See [[concepts/ai-autopilot]]. Taste differentiates only when the muscle is actually used — and polished AI output triggers exactly the cognitive shortcut that bypasses use.

The {{source:parrott-writing-with-ai-harder}}counter-evidence{{/source}} that serious AI-assisted creation *does* exercise the muscle: Parrott's multi-stage writing agent (brainstorm → interview → outline → draft → review) with specialized critic personas — including {{source:parrott-writing-with-ai-harder}}one literally named "Asshole"{{/source}}. The discipline is structural, not vibes-based.

## Practical implication

{{source:sahana-paradigms-agent-space-2026-04-12}}*"If you did not spend enough time in highschool geeking out, engaging in play/ entertainment and building a personality, you should probably get started and go down rabbit holes now."*{{/source}}

The point isn't nostalgia — it's that taste compounds. The references and instincts you absorb early become the raw material your future self (and your future agents) work from.
