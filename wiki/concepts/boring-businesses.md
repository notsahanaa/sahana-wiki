# Boring Businesses That Will Dominate the AI Era

A thesis from Tina He's {{source:tina-he-boring-businesses}}*Every* essay of the same title{{/source}}: when frontier models commoditize, durable advantage no longer comes from algorithms or interfaces. It collects at the *boundaries with reality* — data, compliance, accumulated workflows, regulated industries — i.e. {{source:tina-he-boring-businesses}}*"infrastructure AI must flow through, but cannot replace."*{{/source}}

## The opening provocation

> {{source:tina-he-boring-businesses}}*"At 2:17 a.m., your customer fires you."*{{/source}}

An agent calculates that a $30K SaaS contract delivers $12K of value, and switches without ceremony. {{source:tina-he-boring-businesses}}*"Agents don't need training like human users of software, and they don't have loyalty."*{{/source}} Every UX-derived moat (good design, brand affinity, switching friction) evaporates the moment the customer is an agent.

## Five archetypes of AI-era moats

| Archetype | Moat is | Worked example |
|---|---|---|
| **Knowledge compounders** | Proprietary data that improves with usage | Medal.tv ({{source:tina-he-boring-businesses}}*"700 million gaming clips per year"*{{/source}}, behavioral signal at scale); Mercor (expert human-in-the-loop) |
| **Workflow commons** | Accumulated, production-tested templates | n8n ({{source:tina-he-boring-businesses}}*"7,000+ community-built workflow templates"*{{/source}}, valuation 8× in <12mo) |
| **Reality's gatekeepers** | Compliance/regulatory infrastructure | Stripe, Plaid, Deel, Column (Mercury survived Synapse collapse only by migrating) |
| **Marketplaces** | Routing protocols + accumulated routing intelligence | OpenRouter ({{source:tina-he-boring-businesses}}*"every routed request teaches the platform which models actually perform"*{{/source}}) |
| **Vertical transformers** | End-to-end replacement in regulated industries | Harvey: {{source:tina-he-boring-businesses}}McKinsey's *"$2M three-month review"* → *"48 hours for $20,000"*{{/source}} |

## Memorable claims

- {{source:tina-he-boring-businesses}}*"What endures is infrastructure AI must flow through, but cannot replace."*{{/source}}
- {{source:tina-he-boring-businesses}}*"Trust, once earned, hardens into rebar, painful to rip out and expensive to rebuild."*{{/source}}
- {{source:tina-he-boring-businesses}}*"The dull is what drives the returns. The alternative to paying the toll is building your own bridge."*{{/source}}

## How this sits with the rest of the wiki

This is the **demand-side** counterpart to [[concepts/super-porous-ecosystem]]. Sahana's piece describes the supply side: products as physical compositions of swappable atoms (prompts, tools, skills, MCPs, agents). Tina He answers the obvious next question: *if atoms are interchangeable, where does economic gravity collect?* Answer: at the boundaries the atoms can't dissolve — accumulated data, compliance, workflow templates, domain liability.

Two corollaries the wiki should keep nearby:

1. The **knowledge compounder** archetype is exactly what the wiki itself is, on a personal scale. Every ingest is a clip Medal.tv-style — generated organically as a byproduct of curiosity, hard to replicate from scratch.
2. The **marketplace risk** ({{source:tina-he-boring-businesses}}*"depends entirely on model fragmentation; convergence would collapse the routing value"*{{/source}}) is the right caveat against any "atoms" framing that assumes atoms stay atoms forever.
