# Digital Twins

{{source:digital-twins-clip}}One of the most exciting — and unsettling — things AI enables is digital twins: agents trained on all of a person's publicly available talks, essays, and writing, reconstructed into an interactive presence.{{/source}} Early examples include [Andrew Ng's avatar](https://avatar.andrewng.org) and [a Paul Graham twin](https://www.paulgraham-nia.com/).

## What they promise

The vision is access democratized all the way down. {{source:digital-twins-clip}}*"All the smartest people in today's generation can be codified and regenerated. They can be available as personalised mentors for almost everyone."*{{/source}} The information era made exposure cheap; RAG-based chat makes it interactive and personalized.

Beyond mentorship: as agents become more powerful actors, a digital twin could be instructed to *take actions* as its source person would — not just answer questions but execute strategy, prioritize, negotiate. That's the step from model to clone.

## The unsettling edge

A genuine question sits at the center: {{source:digital-twins-clip}}*"Is a human merely the sum of their thoughts, words, speeches? Is there something more to judgement and intuition that cannot be grasped by these LLMs that train on their output?"*{{/source}} 

The philosophical weight isn't decorative. If a twin can be indistinguishable-enough from the source person in the domains where you'd consult them, it forces a reckoning about what "knowing someone" or "learning from someone" means. It also raises uncomfortable consent and identity questions the field hasn't settled.

## Connection to the one-person studio pattern

The same logic runs in reverse inside [[concepts/one-person-studios]]: rather than reconstructing a famous thinker outward, an individual might encode *themselves* inward — a `soul.md` or `me.md` that carries their voice and values so that agents acting on their behalf actually carry their taste. The wiki itself (`sahana-wiki`) is a precursor artifact.

Digital twins are the public-facing version of a pattern that also has a private-facing version: clones of others for mentorship; clones of self for delegation.
