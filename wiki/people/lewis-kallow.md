# Lewis Kallow

Writer who partners with startup founders on content strategy. Author of the *Action Digest* newsletter; ghostwriter for industry-leading founders; currently developing a consumer AI product. Contributed to [[projects/every]]'s *Playtesting* column with the piece this wiki indexes as [[concepts/social-dandelions]].

## Why he's in this wiki

Kallow's {{source:kallow-social-dandelions}}essay "AI Can Build Anything. Social Dandelions Decide What Spreads."{{/source}} is the wiki's sharpest articulation so far of *why* technical excellence isn't enough in the agent-native era. {{source:kallow-social-dandelions}}*"The bottleneck is now sociology. The founders and creators who will define the next generation are those who understand the invisible physics of how groups of people decide to trust something new."*{{/source}}

His specific contributions to the wiki's vocabulary:

- **Simple vs. complex contagions** as the lens for product adoption (drawn from a 2024 *Nature Communications* review).
- **Wide bridges** as the network structure complex ideas need to cross.
- **Social dandelions** as the persona to target inside a community.
- **Adoption cost / friction-removal** as the third leg, with concrete examples (ChatGPT vs. OpenAI Playground; Granola's silent recording).
